Web / Product Design

MusicHub is a mobile first music discovery product designed to help users explore artists, events, and music experiences in one focused place.

View Live Demo View Github Watch Walkthrough
Overview

A focused way to discover music experiences

I created MusicHub to make discovering music, artists, and local events feel more intuitive and visually engaging. Instead of jumping between different platforms, MusicHub gives users a focused place to explore music related content.

Problem

Music discovery can feel scattered

Finding artists, events, and music related updates can feel inconsistent. Users often move between streaming apps, social media, ticketing sites, and search engines. I wanted to explore how a dedicated mobile experience could make that process feel more direct and enjoyable.

Goal

Design a simple music product with a clear purpose

My goal was to design a product that made music discovery feel focused, easy to browse, and visually engaging.

  • Make music discovery easier to navigate
  • Help users explore artists and events quickly
  • Keep the interface clean, visual, and easy to scan
Process

Step by step product process

1

Defining the idea

I started by identifying the core concept behind MusicHub. I wanted to create something centered around music discovery, but with a cleaner and more curated feel than traditional search or social platforms.

I focused on what problem the product was solving, who it was for, and what would make the experience feel different.

2

Establishing the core experience

Once the idea was clear, I narrowed the product down to its most important actions. This helped keep the product focused instead of overbuilt.

  • Browse artists and music content
  • Explore events and featured experiences
  • Save or revisit music related discoveries
3

Planning the user flow

I built the flow around a simple sequence: open, browse, select, and explore. This helped me think through what users needed first and how the interface could guide them without feeling overwhelming.

4

Designing the interface in Figma

I designed MusicHub in Figma with a visual and modern direction. I focused on strong imagery, card based layouts, clear hierarchy, and spacing that helped music content feel easy to browse.

5

Turning the design into a working frontend

After the visual design phase, I translated the interface into code using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. This helped me test how the design worked beyond static screens.

Takeaways

What I learned

MusicHub helped me understand how a visual product experience can support discovery, browsing, and stronger content organization.

I strengthened my ability to define a focused product concept.
I practiced designing visual, content driven mobile interfaces.
I translated design decisions into real frontend code.
I learned how music content can be structured for easier discovery.
Final Outcome

A polished music discovery experience

MusicHub became a strong project because it reflects both visual design and product thinking. It shows how I approach structure, browsing behavior, interface design, and refinement across the full process.
